Cartagena, Colombia

Official Flag of Colombia

Cartagena de Indias is a coastal gem known for its impeccably preserved colonial history. Founded in 1533, the 'Walled City' is a UNESCO World Heritage site featuring 11km of stone fortifications designed to protect the city from pirate attacks. It is home to the Castillo San Felipe de Barajas—the most formidable fortress ever built by the Spanish in the Americas. Today, it is a vibrant center of Colombian culture, famous for its colorful bougainvillea-draped balconies, emerald trade, and tropical Caribbean charm.
